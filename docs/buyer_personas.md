#### Pain Points (Priority Matrix)

| Priority | Pain Point | Impact | Frequency |
|----------|-----------|--------|-----------|

| **Critical** | Updating tickets feels like busywork | Medium | Daily |
| **High** | Unclear priorities - what should I work on next? | High | Daily |
| **High** | PR reviews stuck without visibility | Medium | Daily |
| **Medium** | Stand-ups are status theater | Low | Daily |
| **Medium** | Can't see how my work connects to goals | Medium | Weekly |

#### Buying Behavior

**Role in Decision:**
- Evaluator during trial period
- Provides feedback to decision maker
- Can veto tools that hurt productivity
- Influences team adoption rate

**Evaluation Criteria:**
1. Does it integrate with my IDE?
2. Can I update tickets without leaving terminal/GitHub?
3. Is the UI fast and not cluttered?
4. Does it auto-sync with my commits/PRs?
5. Can I customize my workflow?

**Adoption Signals:**
- Voluntary use after day 1
- Positive Slack comments to team
- Creates custom workflows
- Recommends to friends at other companies

**Dealbreakers:**
- Slow loading times
- Required fields that don't make sense
- No dark mode
- Forces workflow changes

#### Quote

> "If I have to leave my IDE to update a ticket, I just won't do it. But if my PR automatically links to the story and updates status? That's magic. That's what I'll actually use."

#### Day-in-the-Life Scenario

**9:00 AM** - Maya opens laptop, checks Slack, then opens 4 tabs: GitHub, Jira, Figma, and the codebase. Already dreading the context switching ahead.

**9:15 AM** - Stand-up. Spends 2 minutes trying to remember what ticket number she worked on yesterday. Jira search is slow. Gives vague update.

**10:00 AM** - Deep work on a feature. Gets into flow state. Finishes a chunk of work but doesn't update the ticket because she'd lose focus.

**12:00 PM** - Tech lead Slacks: "What's the status on PROJ-142?" Maya has to stop, find the ticket, remember what she did, and type an update. Flow state: destroyed.

**2:30 PM** - Opens PR. Manually copies ticket link into description. Forgets to move ticket to "In Review." Reviewer later asks "is this ready for review?" because ticket still shows "In Progress."

**4:00 PM** - Sprint ends tomorrow. Maya realizes she has 3 tickets "In Progress" but 2 are actually done - she just forgot to update them. Makes her velocity look bad.

**5:30 PM** - Fills out weekly status report. Copy-pastes from PRs, tickets, and Slack. Thinks: "A good tool would generate this automatically."
